My journey

o-o

Yep. This was where I started out. I remember randomly getting the courage to post my art on youtube - I've thought about doing it for a while, contemplating, being held Back by the fear of being judged.

~ Around August 2024 ~

I figured that the only way to combat the fear was to expose myself to it constantly. So I subjected myself to a 100 day drawing challenge.

I liked the feel and simplicity of the soft graphite pencil, on my sketchbook page, as I drew faces - and soon, hands (oh no! the artists' worst nightmare).

I found motivation in drawing the things I liked. At the time, it was anime.

I'm gonna let you in on a little secret just between the 2 of us. I never finished the 100 day challenge :/ So I failed. But I'd say I needed to because I couldn't stay consistent with waking up at 5am everyday to draw. There was just- no way.

I did learn a ton of lessons from the experience though. The most important was that "if I can do it once, I can do it again". The more you do something, the easier it gets. The fear disappears.
